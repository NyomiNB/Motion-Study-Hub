What is the Pomodoro Technique?

Did you know Pomodoro is actually Itallian for "tomato"? You may be wondering, "Did a farmer develop this technique?" or maybe a chef? The Pomodoro Technique was actually a study method originating from software engineer, Francesco Crillo, during his time as a university student. Crillio was looking for a way to manage his time efficiently, and discovered this method of studying with nothing but his tomato-shaped kitchen timer, hence the name "Pomodoro".

How to Use the Pomodoro Technique

It's simple really! Each task is called a "pomodoro." Each "pomodoro" gets allocated 25 minutes. After the 25 minutes is up, take a 5-minute break. You repeat this process until you have completed 4 pomodoros, or a total of an hour and twenty minutes of work time. After the fourth pomodoro, you take a longer 15-minute break and repeat the process until you've completed your tasks!

Helpful Hints

  • Use the planning page to keep track of deadlines and tasks you'd like to get done!
  • Make sure to actually take a break during breaks! No working allowed!
  • Make sure to stay hydrated and stretch in between pomodoros
